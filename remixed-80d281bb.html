Cockpit Layout
// P2002 Sierra (RP-C1886, RP-C1993) & P2002 JF (RP-C1971/72/75, RP-C4604/05)
Flight Instruments BOTH
Airspeed Indicator (ASI)
Shows aircraft airspeed. White arc: flap operating range. Green: normal cruise. Yellow: caution. Red: never exceed.
LEFT PANEL · TOP LEFT
Attitude Indicator (AI)
Shows aircraft pitch and bank. Gyroscopic instrument. Blue = sky, brown = ground.
LEFT PANEL · CENTER
Altimeter
Shows altitude in feet. Must be set with current altimeter setting (QNH). Kollsman window for pressure setting.
LEFT PANEL · TOP RIGHT
Turn Coordinator (TC)
Shows rate of turn and coordination (ball). Ball centered = coordinated flight. Electrical instrument.
LEFT PANEL · BOTTOM LEFT
Heading Indicator (HI)
Shows magnetic heading. Gyroscopic — must be aligned with magnetic compass. Subject to precession.
LEFT PANEL · BOTTOM CENTER
Vertical Speed Indicator (VSI)
Shows rate of climb/descent in feet per minute. Pneumatic instrument. Slight lag during rapid changes.
LEFT PANEL · BOTTOM RIGHT
Magnetic Compass
Primary heading reference. Subject to magnetic deviation. Unreliable in turns and acceleration/deceleration.
TOP CENTER · GLARE SHIELD
Suction Gauge
Shows vacuum pressure for gyroscopic instruments (AI, HI). Normal range: 4.5–5.5 in Hg. Green arc required.
LEFT PANEL · UPPER LEFT
Engine Instruments BOTH
Tachometer (RPM)
Shows engine RPM. Rotax 912S2. Max continuous: 5500 RPM. Max T/O (5 min): 5800 RPM.
RIGHT PANEL · TOP
Oil Pressure Indicator
Monitor during flight. Normal: 2–7 bar. Min: 0.8 bar → LAND. Max: 7 bar → reduce power.
RIGHT PANEL · UPPER
Oil Temperature Indicator
Normal: 90–130°C. Min: 50°C before takeoff. Max: 130°C. Allow warmup to 50°C at 1000–1200 RPM.
RIGHT PANEL · UPPER
Cylinder Head Temp (CHT)
Monitor cylinder head temperature. Max 135°C. If exceeded: reduce power, land. Engine marked SECURE.
RIGHT PANEL · CENTER
Fuel Pressure
Min: 0.15 bar (2.2 psi). Normal: 0.4 bar (5.8 psi) max. Low fuel pressure = turn on electric fuel pump.
RIGHT PANEL
Fuel Tank Indicators
Left and right tank fuel quantities. Total capacity: 100L (99L usable, 1L unusable total). 50L per tank.
RIGHT PANEL · CENTER
Electrical Instruments BOTH
Voltmeter
Normal: 12–14V. Min (red line): 10.5V. If generator light illuminates (above 16V) → shut down alternator.
RIGHT PANEL
Ammeter
Shows electrical load. Monitor with voltmeter. Battery can operate for 20 minutes if generator fails.
RIGHT PANEL
Outside Air Temperature (OAT)
Monitors outside air temperature. Used for density altitude calculations and icing assessments (OAT <10°C with moisture).
RIGHT PANEL · LOWER
Flap Indicator
Shows current flap position: UP, T/O, FULL. Each setting changes stall speed, lift, and drag characteristics.
RIGHT PANEL · TOP
Chronometer
Elapsed time display. Start at engine start to monitor flight time, fuel consumption, and timing maneuvers.
CENTER PANEL
Trim Indicator
Shows pitch trim position. Set to NEUTRAL for normal takeoff. Trim reduces control pressure on the stick.
TOP LEFT · PANEL
Controls & Switches BOTH
Master Switch & Generator
Master switch powers all electrical. Generator switch must also be ON during flight. Both off during engine securing.
LOWER LEFT PANEL
Ignition Switch
Positions: OFF, L, R, BOTH, START. Always set to BOTH for flight. Check mag drop during run-up (max 130 RPM drop).
LOWER CENTER
Electric Fuel Pump Switch
Backup fuel delivery. ON for: engine start, takeoff, landing, and when low fuel pressure occurs in flight.
LOWER PANEL
Carburetor Heat
Prevents/removes carb ice. Use when below green arc or in visible moisture with OAT <10°C. Expected RPM drop then recovery.
LOWER RIGHT
Throttle & Friction
Main throttle controls power (RPM). Throttle friction lock prevents creep. Rotax 912S2 — liquid cooled.
CENTER CONSOLE
Flaps Selector Knob
Rotary selector: UP, T/O, FULL. Deploy only within white arc (30–67 kts). Each position affects lift and drag.
CENTER CONSOLE / LOWER
Avionics BOTH
Radio (COM)
Communications radio. Primary frequency for RPT-20 area: Binalonan Radio. Emergency: 121.5 MHz.
CENTER STACK
Transponder
Code 1200 for VFR. ALT mode for altitude encoding. Squawk 7700 for emergencies on Class G airspace.
CENTER STACK
Intercom
Pilot-passenger communication. Also interfaces with radio for transmission. Check before flight.
CENTER STACK TOP
Avionics Master P2002JF ONLY
Controls power to avionics stack. NOTE: RP-C1886 and RP-C1993 (Sierra) do NOT have an avionics master switch.

Emergency Procedures
// A.B.C.D.E Framework · P2002JF · Tap to expand
A.B.C.D.E — In-Flight Engine Failure
A
Airspeed 70 kts
Best Glide Speed. Establishes maximum glide range. Do this IMMEDIATELY.
B
Best Place to Land
W7S or COWLS: Wind · Surface · Slope · Size · Civilizations · Obstructions · Winds · Length · Surface
C
Checklist for Restart
C – Carb Heat ON · E – Electric Fuel Pump ON · F – Fuel Quantity CHECK · F – Fuel Selector SWITCH · M – Magnetos BOTH THEN START · T – Throttle AS NEEDED
D
Distress Call
Transponder: SQUAWK 7700 · Radio: SWITCH TO 121.5
Call: "MAYDAY MAYDAY MAYDAY, RP-C____, TECNAM P2002JF, ENGINE FAILURE, NOW OVER ___, ALT ___, WILL MAKE FORCED LANDING ON ___, 2 PERSONS ON BOARD"
E
Engine Securing
T – Throttle IDLE OUT · M – Magnetos OFF · G – Generator OFF · F – Fuel Selector OFF · E – Electric Fuel Pump OFF · Master Switch STANDBY · Flaps AS NEEDED
Below 500 ft: Master OFF, touch down slightly tail-low, apply brakes heavily.

Preliminary Crew Briefing (Abort Plan)
This briefing is delivered BEFORE every takeoff.
If malfunction BEFORE rotate
At your command STOP → Close throttle → Stop aircraft → No actions until complete stop and parking brakes set → Magnetos OFF, Fuel Selector OFF, Fuel Pump OFF, Generator and Master OFF
If malfunction AFTER rotate
Keep speed above stall (50+ kts) → Best place to land (field on right) → Flaps as needed → Throttle IDLE → Magnetos OFF → Fuel Selector OFF → Fuel Pump OFF → Generator OFF → Master STANDBY
If malfunction above 400 ft
Keep 70 kts → Attempt restart: Fuel Pump ON, Change tank, Magnetos BOTH then START, throttle as needed → If unsuccessful: Emergency landing
Transfer of Controls
Command: "MY CONTROLS" → Response: "YOUR CONTROLS" · Positive transfer only.

P2002 JF

COM/NAV radio unit and audio panel. Manages radio communications and navigation signals.
